Prefer not to route through our server? Use your own Strava app (~2 min)

Totally optional — the button above is all most people need. But if you'd rather keep the sign-in 100% between your browser and Strava (or the shared connection is at capacity), Strava lets anyone create a free personal "API app". With it, nothing ever touches our server and nothing outlives this visit.

  1. Go to strava.com/settings/api (logged in as the runner) and create an app — any name and website work.
  2. Set Authorization Callback Domain to this site's domain.
  3. Copy the Client ID and Client Secret into the boxes below and connect.

Add your glucose data
Dexcom: how to export from Clarity (~30 sec)
  1. In a browser, log in at clarity.dexcom.com — the Clarity phone app can't export CSV, but this works fine in a phone browser too.
  2. On any report page, click the export icon (top of the page) and pick a date range covering the day of the run.
  3. Click Export — you get a .csv file. Choose it below.
FreeStyle Libre: how to export from LibreView (~30 sec)
  1. In a browser, log in at libreview.com with your Libre account.
  2. Click Download glucose data (top right of the page) and confirm — you get a .csv file covering your recent readings.
  3. Choose that file below.
Another meter or app?

Drop its CSV export in anyway — the reader also understands any spreadsheet with a time column and a glucose column (Medtronic CareLink exports usually work). Whatever the device: the file never leaves your browser; nothing is uploaded.
